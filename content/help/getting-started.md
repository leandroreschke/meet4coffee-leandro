# Getting Started with Meet 4 Coffee

Welcome! This guide will walk you through setting up your workspace and running your first coffee club round.

## Step 1: Create Your Workspace

After signing up, you'll create your organization:

1. **Name**: Choose something your team will recognize
2. **Slug**: This becomes your URL (e.g., `meet4coffee.com/w/your-team`)
3. **Timezone**: Pick your primary team's timezone
4. **Workday Hours**: Define when coffee chats should typically happen

## Step 2: Invite Your Team

Send invite links to your team members:

1. Go to **Config > Invites**
2. Create an invite link
3. Share it in Slack/Teams
4. Team members click and join automatically

## Step 3: Create Your First Club

Clubs are groups of people who meet regularly:

1. Go to **Clubs**
2. Click **Create Club**
3. Configure your settings:
   - **Name**: Something descriptive (e.g., "Weekly Coffee Chat")
   - **Frequency**: Weekly, biweekly, or monthly
   - **Group Size**: Pairs (2) or small groups (3-4)
   - **Duration**: 30 minutes is typical

## Step 4: Add Members to Your Club

1. From your club page, click **Manage Members**
2. Select team members to invite
3. They'll receive notifications about the club

## Step 5: Schedule Your First Round

1. In your club, click **New Round**
2. Pick the anchor date (first meeting day)
3. Meet 4 Coffee generates matchups automatically
4. Review and confirm

## What Happens Next

Once confirmed:
- Calendar invites go out automatically (if Google Calendar is connected)
- Slack notifications are sent (if integrated)
- Participants get meeting links

## Tips for Your First Round

### Start Small
Begin with a single club of enthusiastic participants. Early wins build momentum.

### Be Flexible
Expect some scheduling conflicts. People can reschedule or opt-out—it's normal.

### Gather Feedback
After the first round, ask participants: "How did it go? What should we change?"

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I run multiple clubs?**
Yes! Many teams have different clubs for different purposes (engineering + design, random cross-team, leadership, etc.)

**Q: What if someone misses their chat?**
Life happens. They can catch up in the next round or schedule manually.

**Q: How are matchups decided?**
Our algorithm rotates pairings to ensure variety while respecting opt-out preferences.
